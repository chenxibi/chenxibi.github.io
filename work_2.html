ARISE

An AR card game with music therapy elements designed for autism children and their therapists, made during the module Serious Game at Universit√© Paris-Saclay.

Objective: We believe that this game could improve communication skills of children with autism.

Mechanics: The game uses AR technology to allow children to play music, with the addition of card exchange sessions and thematic play narratives such as a garden or a train. The major storyline of the game is for players to play the sheet and fix the virtual world with the cards collected through their sheet performance. In sessions, patients receive instrument cards and a stick, matching the therapist's equipment. Scanning a card displays the instrument, and sliding the stick plays notes. Some cards have preset melodies, and successfully replicating them allows users to claim and save the card. Blank cards enable users to create and record their own melodies for later use. Each melody card corresponds to a theme-based model with animations chosen by the therapist. Users can combine 5 or more cards to build a garden or train circuit. Card variety encourages interaction in autism therapy, promoting trade between patients and therapists. Uneven card distribution encourages patient interaction, and easy card production fosters motivation and interest in therapy.

Themes: We currently have two themes - garden and train - for therapists to choose when they begin the therapy. Due to the time limit, we have only made a prototype for the garden theme, as presented in the video below. For the train theme, the cards that players can collect through sheet performance are parts and facilities of the train such as the engine, the restaurant wagon, the passenger wagon, and the cargo wagon.

Ageing

Coursework for Creative Design at UP Saclay

In the next 3 seconds, please think of a word to describe older people.
Now please have a look at the poster. Is your word in it or close to one of them?
The ageing population is often labelled as a social problem in not only HCI research but also in everyday lives - they suffer from a variety of health issues, have physical and cognitive impairments, have difficulty using technology, and experience social isolation and loss of independence. Such a discourse can be stigmatizing and could hurt older adults’ dignity, making them feel less worthy and esteemed.
This graphic design work, therefore, aims to raise the audience’s awareness of everyday ageism. The words in the poster are all commonly used when designers, caregivers … and the media talk about older people. Through this poster, the audience is encouraged to reflect on their own bias, and as a result, avoid using the discourses of decline and dependency when talking about ageing from now on to make this society more inclusive to older people.

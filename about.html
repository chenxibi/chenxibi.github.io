About

Started in 2022, Age Smartly is an organisation that works to promote positive changes in society to help improve the living conditions of older adults.

 

What we know

The digital divide is a global problem. It refers to the inequality between those who are and who are not literate in using the internet for information and entertainment. Older adults often have difficulty using the internet. As a result, their lives are largely inconvenienced in today’s digital era when public services and lives are increasingly online.

 

Moreover, seniors often feel bored and lonely. Many have stated that they can’t find purpose in life after retirement. This has become worse since the pandemic began. The problem of loneliness and social isolation is not entirely the result of the digital divide, nor can easily be solved by the internet. However, we believe by increasing the percentage of seniors using the internet, they will live a less lonely and more fulfilling life.

 

What we do

Our main aim is to remove the barriers that potentially prevent seniors from useing the internet, which mainly come from society instead of themselves. We produce explainer articles, advice and guidance to policy makers, tech professionals and the public, to raise awareness of the contemporary living conditions of seniors.

 

For example, one important cause of the digital divide is the tendency of professionals in the tech industry to ignore the needs of the older audience when making their products. To make a change, we will produce overviews of accessibility literature to guide designers and developers.

 

Besides, we also have exciting events, particularly for seniors that centre on a variety of topics, from film screenings to participatory design workshops.
